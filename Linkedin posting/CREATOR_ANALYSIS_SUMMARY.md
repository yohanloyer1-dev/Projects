## The Most Important Finding: 5 Hook Formulas That Actually Work

### Formula 1 — Problem Statement + Data (Eli Weiss model)
> "Try X on your own site. Most never do this. Here's why: [statistic]"

**Best for:** Educational CX posts, metric deep-dives, Gorgias feature posts
**Engagement level:** HIGH (18–47 reactions average)
**Yohan application:** "Pull up your Gorgias dashboard right now. Look at your TPO. Most brands have never done this..."

---

### Formula 2 — Contrarian Opinion (Jeremy Horowitz / Davie Fogarty model)
> "X is happening and [industry] doesn't realize Y"

**Best for:** Reframe posts, hot takes, CX-as-revenue positioning
**Engagement level:** HIGH (sparks debate, drives comments)
**Yohan application:** "Most e-commerce brands don't have a retention problem. They have a support problem." ← Already used in POST 01

---

### Formula 3 — Story Setup (Nick Shackelford model)
> "I was in a conversation recently with [type of person]. I noticed something..."

**Best for:** Insider observation posts, agency/operator real talk
**Engagement level:** MEDIUM-HIGH (draws people in, feels exclusive)
**Yohan application:** "Had a call last week with an agency building their first Gorgias offer. I asked one question. Their answer told me everything..."

---

### Formula 4 — Insider Claim (works across all creators)
> "I've never seen [X] without [Y]. The correlation is too consistent to ignore."

**Best for:** Credibility-building posts, CX pattern recognition
**Engagement level:** HIGH (creates intrigue, demands explanation)
**Yohan application:** "I've never seen a brand with a broken Gorgias setup have a strong retention rate. Not once."

---

### Formula 5 — Direct Challenge (Jeremy Horowitz model)
> "Stop doing X. Here's what to do instead."

**Best for:** Automation posts, advice-driven content, quick takes
**Engagement level:** HIGH when contrarian, MEDIUM when generic
**Yohan application:** "Stop hiring more support agents. Fix the system first."  ← Already used in POST 11

## What Drives Engagement (Ranked)

1. **Data-backed statistics** — specific numbers that feel surprising or validating
2. **Actionable tests** — things readers can apply TODAY, not "strategy"
3. **Contrarian opinions** — respectful disagreement with conventional wisdom
4. **Personal storytelling** — specific anonymized scenarios, not generic
5. **Philosophical reframes** — changing how someone sees a problem
6. **Community/exclusivity** — limited access, insider knowledge

---

## What Gets Shared (vs. What Just Gets Liked)

Posts that get **shared/reposted** have one of:
- A unique operator perspective that others want their network to see
- Actionable advice in a simple framework (lists, formulas, systems)
- A strong contrarian take that makes the sharer look smart

Posts that only get **likes** are typically:
- Personal life updates (warm network, doesn't grow reach)
- Company announcements (low engagement rate across all creators)
- Generic motivational content

**Implication for Yohan:** His paternity post (156 comments) went wide with his warm network but didn't build CX authority. His Gorgias Chat 2.0 post (10 comments, 3,882 impressions) was company marketing in his voice. Neither is the target content type. The 20 drafted posts hit the right zone.

## What NOT To Do (From Creator Mistakes Observed)

- **Don't post company announcements in your personal voice** — it reads as marketing, not thought leadership. Every post should sound like YOU, not Gorgias PR.
- **Don't post without a clear POV** — "interesting news from Gorgias!" is a press release, not a post
- **Don't use excessive emoji stacks** — Yohan's recent posts use 🚀🎉💡 chains that read as corporate content
- **Don't start with "I"** — LinkedIn algorithm deprioritises posts that begin with "I"
- **Don't ask for engagement explicitly** — "What do you think? Drop a comment!" feels hollow

---

## The Engagement Timing Pattern

All 5 creators showed the same pattern:
- **Hours 0–2:** Critical window. Engagement in this period tells the algorithm to distribute the post more widely.
- **Pre-engagement** (commenting before you post): Most effective growth hack. Comment on posts by your target audience right before you publish your own.
- **Reply to every comment** in the first few hours — each reply counts as activity and resets the distribution window.

## Newsletter Observation

All 5 creators have a newsletter or email list. This is non-negotiable for long-term authority. LinkedIn followers = rented audience. Email list = owned audience. The agency play also depends on having a direct channel to potential clients. Priority: Start a newsletter in parallel with or shortly after the LinkedIn strategy launches.
