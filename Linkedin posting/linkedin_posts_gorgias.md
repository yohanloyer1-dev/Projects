## POST 2 — The metric nobody talks about: TPO

Everyone's obsessed with CSAT.

Barely anyone tracks Tickets Per Order.

Here's why TPO is the most underrated metric in e-commerce CX:

It tells you how much friction your post-purchase experience creates per transaction. High TPO = product issues, unclear comms, broken logistics, or a returns process your customers hate.

Low TPO = operational excellence. Fewer tickets means fewer problems — and that's revenue you keep instead of burning on support costs.

CSAT tells you how your agents performed. TPO tells you how your *brand* performed.

If you're only measuring one, you're flying half-blind.

## POST 9 — The real cost of wrong-email WISMO

"Where is my order?" is the single most common support ticket in e-commerce.

Gorgias AI Agent handles it automatically. That part is known.

What wasn't: when a customer emailed from a different address than the one on the order, the AI would pull up the wrong order data — and confidently tell the customer the wrong thing.

That's now fixed. The Trust System verifies identity across email addresses before surfacing order info.

Why does this matter beyond the obvious?

Because a confident wrong answer is worse than no answer. It erodes trust in a way that generic "I don't know" never does.

Getting AI right in support isn't just about automation rate. It's about accuracy under edge cases.

## POST 17 — The Knowledge Hub toggle that changes everything

One of the most underrated controls in Gorgias AI Agent:

The ability to toggle which knowledge sources the AI uses — and critically, to *exclude* it from using certain information independently.

Why does this matter?

Because not all knowledge is equal. Your AI knowing your return policy is great. Your AI independently deciding how to handle an exception to that policy — without a human in the loop — is risky.

The Knowledge Hub independence toggle gives CX managers surgical control: "Use this to inform responses, but never act on it autonomously."

The brands that get AI governance right early will have far fewer AI-related customer incidents. This is how you deploy AI confidently instead of cautiously.

## POST 20 — What I've learned watching brands implement Gorgias AI Agent

The ones who fail: they deploy and walk away.
The ones who succeed: they treat it like onboarding a new team member.

Week 1: Give it your knowledge. Policies, FAQs, product docs. The more context, the better the outputs.

Week 2: Watch the Reasoning logs. Understand *why* it's saying what it's saying. Correct the edge cases.

Week 3: Add Actions. Start with WISMO and basic cancellations. Measure the automation rate lift.

Month 2: Use Auto QA to spot patterns. Is AI Agent consistent? Is it empathetic? Where is it failing at the same spot repeatedly?

Month 3: You're not managing a tool. You're coaching an agent.

The mindset shift is everything. AI Agent isn't a product you configure once — it's a team member you develop.

The brands that get this are the ones generating real ROI.
