# LinkedIn Profile Optimization Brief
*For: Yohan Loyer | Analyzed: March 2026 | Do this BEFORE publishing your first authority post*

---

## Why This Matters

Your profile is a landing page. Every post you publish sends people to it.
Right now, someone landing on your profile after reading a sharp CX post would find:
- A job title as a headline
- A corporate About section written for recruiters
- A 3-year-old farewell post pinned as Featured
- No clear signal of what you stand for or why to follow you

You have 5,945 followers. They followed you for some reason — mostly your Gorgias proximity. The strategy is to convert that passive audience into engaged followers who see you as the authority. The profile needs to match the content you're about to publish.

---

## ELEMENT 1: Headline

**Current:**
> EMEA Agency Partnerships @Gorgias

**Problem:** This is a job title. It answers "what do you do at Gorgias" not "why should I follow you." It blends you into every other Partnership Manager on LinkedIn.

**Goal headline:** Should immediately tell a stranger: (1) what you're an expert in, (2) who you help, and (3) hint at your POV.

**Option A — Authority positioning (recommended):**
> Gorgias specialist | Turning e-commerce support into a revenue channel | EMEA

**Option B — Problem-solving framing:**
> Helping e-commerce brands stop losing customers they already paid to acquire | Gorgias EMEA

**Option C — Practitioner voice:**
> 5 years inside Gorgias. I know what the best e-commerce CX setups look like — and what breaks them.

**Option D — Cleanest/shortest:**
> E-commerce CX & Gorgias | What good support actually looks like | EMEA

**My recommendation:** Option A. It names your niche (Gorgias), names the outcome (revenue), and signals geography. Clean, credible, differentiated.

**Character limit:** LinkedIn headline = 220 characters. All options above are well within limit.

## ELEMENT 2: About Section

**Current (full text):**
> "After 3.5 years at Salesforce, I joined the #1 helpdesk for eCommerce merchants, Gorgias. As a Partner Manager, I help our partners understand and share the value of Gorgias with their customers to help them provide a great customer support experience, and turn customer support into a profit center.
>
> If you have any questions or would like to get my thoughts on Gorgias, Customer Service, or Customer Experience, I'm more than happy to help 😊
>
> I am also passionate about E-commerce, Entrepreneurship, Customer Experience, new technologies, Web3, and personal development. Feel free to reach out, and let's discuss how we can help each other out!"

**Problems:**
- Opens with your career story (nobody cares about that until they're already interested in you)
- Written for recruiters, not for your target audience (e-commerce operators/founders/CX managers)
- "I'm more than happy to help" is passive — it doesn't give a reason to follow you
- The Web3 mention is off-brand and dated
- No clear statement of what you post about or what value you deliver to followers

**Rewritten About section:**

---

*Suggested rewrite (paste directly):*

```
I've spent 5+ years inside Gorgias — the e-commerce helpdesk used by 15,000+ brands.

Before that, 3.5 years in enterprise sales at Salesforce. I understand how support is sold. Now I understand how it actually works.

Here's what that gives me:
I've seen hundreds of e-commerce CX setups — what separates the brands that retain customers from the ones that lose them after the first order. I know which automations actually reduce ticket volume and which ones just frustrate customers. I know what "good" looks like in Gorgias — and more importantly, what breaks.

I write about:
→ CX as a revenue lever (not a cost centre)
→ Gorgias playbooks, setups, and what top brands do differently
→ CS automation and efficiency
→ Retention, loyalty, and the psychology of why customers stay or leave

If you work in e-commerce — as a founder, operator, CX lead, or agency — this is the feed to follow.

📍 Cannes, France | EMEA
```

---

**Why this works:**
- Opens with a credibility signal (5+ years, 15,000+ brands)
- Quickly contextualises the Salesforce background as a STRENGTH not just a backstory
- Uses "I know what X looks like" framing — this is authority language, not humble language
- Makes the value proposition explicit: what you post about + who should follow you
- Arrow-formatted list is easy to skim
- Ends with a clear "this is for you if..." statement
