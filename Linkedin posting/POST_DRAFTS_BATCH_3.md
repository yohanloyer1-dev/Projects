## POST 41 — The ROI formula that gets CX teams budget

Your CFO doesn't care about CSAT.

They care about money in vs. money out.

The formula:
Customer Service ROI = (Returns − Costs) / Costs × 100

That's it.

But most CX leaders never run this number. They show up to budget meetings with ticket volume and satisfaction scores, and wonder why they can't get headcount approved.

The brands that win the budget fight — Lovevery, Lush — speak in CLV and retention, not CSAT and response times.

Calculate your ROI. Bring the number. That's how the conversation changes.

## POST 43 — The sales gap nobody talks about

## POST 76 — Cart abandonment vs. checkout abandonment

These are different problems.

Cart abandonment: they added to cart, then left without starting checkout. They're considering, not committing.

Checkout abandonment: they started entering information and stopped. Something went wrong at the finish line.

Cart → fix the consideration (better information, clearer value, educational campaign).
Checkout → fix the friction (hidden fees, forced account creation, missing payment method).

Most brands treat them as the same problem. They're not. The interventions are completely different.

## POST 79 — Upsell vs. cross-sell. Most brands confuse them.

Upsell: higher-value version of what they're already buying.
"Want the 500g bag instead of the 250g?"

Cross-sell: complementary product.
"This pairs well with X."

Both increase AOV. Neither alone is optimal.

The brands with the highest AOV do both, at the right moment: upsell on the product page, cross-sell in the cart and post-purchase.

If you're doing only one of these — or neither — you're leaving a measurable amount of revenue per order on the table.

## POST 90 — CRO happens at every stage. Most brands optimize only the last one.
