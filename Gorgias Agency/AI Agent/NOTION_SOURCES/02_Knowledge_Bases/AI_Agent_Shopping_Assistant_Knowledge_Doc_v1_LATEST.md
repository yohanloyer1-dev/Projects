## 1. <span discussion-urls="{{discussion://1ba1ae21-78f5-8038-b47c-001c31a2a422}}">What is </span>Shopping Assistant {toggle="true"}
	::: callout {color="gray_bg"}
		***Conversation is the new storefront. ***
		- Proactively engage with visitors to reduce drop-off and keep shoppers moving toward purchase.
		- Reduce doubt and educate customers by answering product questions and guiding confident decisions.
		- Maximize cart value with natural, complementary product recommendations.
		- Leverage rich browsing insights such as, cart activity and session behaviour to deliver the most personalized, well-rounded shopping experience.
	:::

## 2. <span discussion-urls="{{discussion://1cf1ae21-78f5-80d8-8196-001ca5a15eb8}}">What are the top 3 value propositions? </span> {toggle="true"}
	<synced_block url="{{https://www.notion.so/1521ae2178f58007a514ef01a3e19191#2231ae2178f58073a49fc4e2c22b8072}}">
		- **Proactively engages shoppers at key moments**, such as on the homepage, during search, and on product pages. It offers helpful information and seamless chat access to ensure they feel supported throughout their journey.
		- **Provide personalized product recommendations** based on real-time shopper data to match individual needs while increasing upsell and cross-sell opportunities.
		- **Determines when a discount is necessary** by evaluating shopper intent, browsing activity, and behavior, helping convert the right customers while protecting your margins.
	</synced_block>

		<summary>**How does the Shopping Assistant identify shopper interest and purchase intent?**</summary>
			# 📊 Purchase Intent Calculation
			## 1. Purpose and Context
			The goal of purchase intent calculation is to **determine where a shopper stands in their buying journey** so the AI Sales Agent can adapt its behavior accordingly. Instead of treating all shoppers the same way, the agent uses **signals from conversation + browsing behavior** to assign a shopper to one of three initial stages:
			- **Discovery** → broad exploration, low intent
			- **Interested** → narrowed product focus, mid intent
			- **Ready to Buy** → transaction-driven, high intent
			This stage then **informs the playbook**: how assertive the agent should be, whether to ask questions or make recommendations, and when to apply urgency or incentives.
			---
			## 2. Inputs Considered
			The calculation relies on **6 key input variables**:
			1. **Conversation with the shopper**
				- Natural language cues: questions about products, objections, checkout, shipping, etc.
				- Example: *“Do you have running shoes?”* → Discovery
				- Example: *“Does this pendant come in rose gold?”* → Interested
				- Example: *“Can I pay with PayPal?”* → Ready to Buy
			2. **Active cart (****`cart_data`****)**
				- Presence of items in cart = strong purchase intent.
				- Cart abandonment or hesitation may also refine stage classification.
			3. **Products viewed (****`top_visited_product_pages`****)**
				- Few product views across multiple categories = early exploration.
				- Multiple views of the same product or variants = deeper interest.
			4. **Pages viewed (****`top_visited_pages`****)**
				- Homepage / collections = Discovery.
				- Shipping, returns, sizing, or checkout pages = Interested / Ready.
			5. **Purchase history (****`order_array`****)**
				- Repeat buyers may move faster through the funnel.
				- Returning customers with prior purchases often jump directly to **Interested** or **Ready**.
			6. **Cart abandonment (****`last_cart_abandoned_at`****)**
				- Shoppers who previously abandoned may need reassurance (discounts, urgency).
				- Influences whether stage = Hesitant or Ready with Friction (planned in V2).
			---
			## 3. Stage Classification Logic (V1)
			The **qualify_interaction:light:check_customer_interest_stage prompt** processes the above inputs and classifies the shopper into one of **three stages**:
			### **Discovery**
			- **Definition**: Shopper is exploring broadly, not tied to specific products.
			- **Signals**:
				- Broad / vague questions: “I need a gift,” “Looking for red nails.”
				- Pages: homepage, multiple collections.
				- No cart activity.
			- **Agent response**: Ask clarifying questions, educate, guide gently.
			### **Interested**
			- **Definition**: Shopper has product-specific questions and shows clear pre-purchase behavior.
			- **Signals**:
				- Asking about details (size, color, shipping).
				- Viewing the same product multiple times.
				- Reading return/shipping policies.
			- **Agent response**: Recommend specific products, surface product cards, address objections, possibly offer discounts.
			### **Ready to Buy**
			- **Definition**: Shopper signals transactional intent.
			- **Signals**:
				- Items in cart, checkout page viewed.
				- Asking about payment methods, shipping fees, discounts.
				- Returning customer with intent to reorder.
			- **Agent response**: Remove friction, provide direct product links, encourage checkout completion.
			---
			## 4. How the AI Uses the Output
			Once a stage is assigned, it is **passed into the generate prompt**, which determines **Sales Playbook behavior**:
			- **Stage + Sales Assertiveness Settings = Conversation Style**
				- Discovery + Educational → Ask refining questions.
				- Discovery + Assertive → Move quicker to recommendations.
				- Ready to Buy overrides assertiveness (agent prioritizes checkout help).
			This ensures alignment between **customer readiness** and **merchant preferences**.
			---
			## 5. Why It Matters
			- **Personalization at scale**: Each shopper receives guidance aligned with their journey stage.
			- **Higher conversion rates**: Merchants benefit from stage-aware recommendations.
			- **Clarity for AI**: Removes guesswork, reduces hallucinations, enforces structured playbook-driven interactions.
			---
			✅ **In short:** Purchase intent is calculated by combining shopper conversation signals, browsing behavior, cart activity, and history into one of three intent stages. This stage then drives which branch of the Sales Playbook the AI follows, adapting assertiveness and guidance to maximize conversion.

		<summary>**How does each sales persuasion level work? **</summary>
			::: callout {color="gray_bg"}
				Specs DB: <mention-page url="{{https://www.notion.so/1ab1ae2178f580a3a955d3604268baad}}"/>

			Brands have full control over how the AI Agent for Sales engages with shoppers by setting their preferred **Selling Style**. This setting determines how educational and persuasive the AI is in guiding shoppers toward a purchase, ensuring that the conversation aligns with the brand’s sales philosophy.
			Brands can choose from three levels:
			1️⃣ **Educational** – Prioritizes guidance and product education, with minimal sales pressure.
			2️⃣ **Moderate** – Maintains a mix of education and persuasion, encouraging purchases while ensuring a helpful approach.
			3️⃣ **Promotional** – Focuses on closing the sale quickly, using urgency and direct product recommendations.
			By adjusting the **Sales Persuasion Level**, merchants define how the AI interacts at each **Customer Interest Stage**, ensuring that responses are tailored to both the shopper’s behavior and the brand’s selling style.

	<summary>**Q: What’s the difference between Customer Engagement and Convert? **</summary>
		<synced_block_reference url="{{https://www.notion.so/1e71ae2178f580c1b346ed91af2b3436#21f1ae2178f5805181e0f4bee1fcff2b}}">
			## Convert: <span underline="true">Manual</span> Proactive Assistance
			**Convert aka Chat Campaigns** is a manual campaign tool that allows brands to configure custom engagement messages based on their own targeting rules, timing, and copy.
			### Key Attributes of Convert:
			- ❌ No AI involved: All triggers and messaging are manually configured.
			- ✅ Merchants decide exactly when messages appear and what they say.
			- ✅ Ideal for specific promotions, events, email capture, video usage, or sales-led triggers.
			- ❌ Requires ongoing management and optimization by the brand.
			## Customer Engagement (within AI Agent): AI-Driven Automation
			The **Customer Engagement** capabilities under AI Agent are fully AI-powered, offering hands-off, intelligent automation that adapts to real-time customer behavior.
			### Key Attributes of Customer Engagement:
			- <span discussion-urls="{{discussion://1ec1ae21-78f5-809a-a1b5-001cdb2dfd3f}}">✅ </span><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://1ec1ae21-78f5-809a-a1b5-001cdb2dfd3f}}">**AI-driven logic**</span><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://1ec1ae21-78f5-809a-a1b5-001cdb2dfd3f}}">: Automatically identifies the best time, place, and message.</span>
			- ✅ **Smart messaging**: Prompts are dynamically generated based on context and intent.
			- ✅ **Zero configuration needed**: AI Agent handles targeting, timing, and personalization.
			- ❌ Less granular control for brands wanting precise scripting or fixed scheduling.

	<summary><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://24d1ae21-78f5-8044-9634-001ce0d9a29f}}">**Q: How does the recommendation work exactly? **</span></summary>
		The Shopping Assistant suggests relevant products by combining:
		::: callout {color="gray_bg"}
			**TL;DR** - SA will leverage the title, description and variant title to recommend products.
		:::
		1. **LLM query extraction** – analyzes customer conversations to identify product-related queries.
		2. **Query categorization** – tags each as either *identification* (find similar items) or *recommendation* (suggest complementary items).
		3. **ML-based retrieval** – sends queries to the Knowledge Recommender API, which returns product IDs based on similarity and store context.
		4. **Product enrichment** – uses Helpdesk APIs to pull real-time product info, inventory data, and scraped product page content for accuracy and detail. It will only look at title, description and variant title). 
		**Example:**
		If a customer asks, *“Do you have something like the Nimbus 25?”*, the system runs an identification query, retrieves similar products, enriches their details, and shows them to the customer.
		This layered approach ensures **personalized, accurate, and context-aware recommendations** that adapt to each store’s catalog and inventory in real time.

	<summary>**Q: How can I promote or exclude products?** </summary>
		<synced_block_reference url="{{https://www.notion.so/1f51ae2178f580048c32fd7dbafe4f8a#2531ae2178f580d885bfde0dd479da46}}">
			## Promote or Exclude Products {toggle="true"}
				<video src="{{https://www.loom.com/share/2cb0aa8ff70149a69a2e49d0fbc29df3?sid=b5b633de-7878-490f-b2e2-19f9e3981137}}"></video>
				<empty-block/>
				Merchants can now directly customize which products the Shopping Assistant recommends through a set of easy-to-use controls. They can:
				- **Promote specific products or groups of products** by applying tags, vendors, collections or selecting individual products. Promoted products will be prioritized in recommendations when they are relevant to a customer’s query.
				- **Exclude specific products or groups of products** using tags, vendors, collections or products. Excluded products will never be suggested by the Shopping Assistant, except in cases where a customer specifically asks about them or already has them in their cart.
				- **Apply rules dynamically** so that changes to product with a specific tags or vendors are reflected daily in the recommendation logic - no need to update it in Gorgias.
				- **See lists of promoted and excluded products** for transparency and easy management.
				### **Here is how Shopping Assistant will takes the rules into consideration**
				<synced_block_reference url="{{https://www.notion.so/2261ae2178f580229594dca997ffc079#2481ae2178f580c182a9d21d7f9db88e}}">

				::: callout {icon="💡" color="green_bg"}
					The AI Agent uses promotion as an extra signal: it will favor promoted products when relevance is similar, but always prioritizes what’s most relevant to the shopper.

				## Conflicts
				A conflict occurs when a product is both promoted and excluded. For example, when a product has the promoted tag `Running Shoes` and the excluded vendor `Nike` .
				Here’s how we now resolve them:
				- **Same-level conflict - **The tag **`Best Sellers`** is promoted. If a merchant excludes this tag, it will automatically be removed from the promoted rules.
				- **Product vs. group conflict - **The product **`Nike Vomero Premium`** is promoted, but the vendor **`Nike`** is excluded. → The **product rule** takes priority, so the product will still be promoted.
				- **Group vs. group conflict - **The vendor **`Barbie`** is promoted, but the tag **`Dolls`** is excluded. → The **excluded rule** takes priority, so the product will be excluded.
				Badges and tooltips across the interface indicate when conflicts occur, helping merchants understand which rules take effect.
				## Miscellaneous
				---
				- <span underline="true">**Collections**</span>: Because collections are synced once a day, any changes made to collections—such as adding or removing products, or creating new collections—will only be reflected in the Shopping Assistant’s recommendations the following day. This means you won’t be able to update which products are recommended in real time during the day; updates become visible after the next daily sync.

			### How it works <mention-user url="{{user://1f9d872b-594c-8109-8371-00025aeaa944}}"/>  {toggle="true"}
				Shopping Assistant will adapt its answer based on:
				- The Selling Style
				- The Type of question being asked
				- The Customer interest stage (Discovery or Ready to Buy)
				<empty-block/>
				For Discovery the behavior will change depending on the broadness score. If the broadness score is low, it means that very few products match the customer's request. While if the broadness score is high, it means that the query is quite broad and a lot of products could be recommended.  
				<br><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://2a91ae21-78f5-803e-8456-001c4facb98e}}">How is the broadness score calculated? </span>
				- The broadness score tells the AI how specific a shopper’s question is.<br>A **high** score means the request is vague and could **match many products**, while a **low** score means it’s clear and **points to a** **specific product** — guiding whether the AI should ask more questions or start recommending.

				<details>
				<summary>**Why did we make this change? **</summary>
					Previously, Shopping Assistant’s behavior was limited and had minimal differences across the various selling styles (Educational, Balanced, and Promotional).
					Shopping Assistant would also ruthlessly cross-sell without considering what topic was being discussed by the shopper, regardless of if the shopper was displaying buying intent or not. 
					We needed to create a way to adapt Shopping Assistant messaging based on the Selling Style, questions asked, and customer intent. 
				</details>
				<details>
				<summary>**Messaging: **</summary>
					This new update to Shopping Assistant’s behaviors will strengthen our claim across our different value props: 
						<details>
						<summary>**Proactively engages shoppers at key moments**</summary>
							**We claim →** 
							*Shopping Assistant engages visitors to reduce drop-off and keep shoppers moving toward purchase.* *It offers helpful information and seamless chat access to ensure they feel supported throughout their journey.*
							**How this release makes us stronger →** 
							Shopping Assistant is now intelligent enough to adapt its behavior across different stages of the buying journey. By detecting intent, it can engage shoppers with the most relevant follow ups to ensure every reply takes the shopper one step closer to conversion. 
						</details>
						<details>
						<summary>**Educate customers as they shop**</summary>
							**We claim → **
							*Shopping Assistant reduces doubt and educates customers by answering product questions and guiding confident decisions.*
							**How this release makes us stronger →**
							Shopping Assistant no longer defaults to selling in every scenario, and instead will focus on product education if it detects that this is what the shopper is requesting. This helps shoppers make more confident purchasing decisions by helping them access the information they need more easily, instead of defaulting to sending product cards.

				<summary><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://2b01ae21-78f5-8007-aec3-001cc8b2e698}}">✅ Add context to promotions</span></summary>
					::: callout {color="purple_bg"}
						**Context: **Some merchants may have promotions running outside of the discounting features that Shopping Assistant includes. Shopping Assistant should be aware of these external promotions and be able to share the appropriate discount based on customer context. 
					:::
					### When to share promotions with customers 
					**WHEN **the shopper is asking for a discount for the first time 
					- **IF** this is the shopper’s first discount request
						- **THEN:**
							1. Offer the newsletter subscription discount (10%) as the initial promotion.
							2. Use phrasing such as: “You can get 10% off by subscribing to our newsletter.”
							3. Do **not** mention any other promotions at this stage.
					- **IF t**he shopper declines the newsletter offer or insists on another discount
						- **THEN:**
							1. Share **one** applicable promotion from the list below:
								- **Military Discount (15%)** — available with ID verification at checkout.
									*Example phrasing:* “We also offer a 15% military discount — you can apply it at checkout by verifying your military ID.”
								- **Temporary Sale (10%)** — available with code **TEMP10%**, valid until midnight.
									*Example phrasing:* “We currently have a 10% off temporary sale using code TEMP10%, valid until midnight.”
					- **IF** the shopper continues to insist or asks for a better discount after the above promotions
						- **THEN:**
							1. Generate a unique 20% discount code.
							2. Share it with phrasing such as: “I’ve created a 20% discount code for you — you can use this at checkout for extra savings.”

				<summary><span discussion-urls="{{discussion://2b01ae21-78f5-8007-aec3-001cc8b2e698}}">✅ Influence discovery questions</span></summary>
					::: callout {color="purple_bg"}
						**Context: **In some cases, merchants may want to ask specific discovery questions for certain items. Shopping Assistant will be able to make a more accurate recommendation for customers if it asks the correct questions, and we can use Guidance to specify what to ask. 
					:::
					### When a shopper is looking for running shoes, ask these discovery questions
					**WHEN** a customer asks for running shoe
					1. Always ask those 2 questions, one by one, not simultaneously
						- How many miles per week do you run?
						- What type of terrain do you run on?
					2. Based on answer of step 1
						- **IF **they run more than 5 miles, ask the following
							- How many times per week do you run?
						- **IF **they run less than 5 miles, tell them the following
							- You need to run more to leverage the shoes we sell, go to Decathlon
					**THEN**:
					- Summarize what you’ve learned (e.g.: “So you run 15 miles/week, mostly on roads, training for a half marathon…”)
					- Recommend 1–2 best-fit models.
					- If the profile doesn’t fit your range, be transparent and redirect accordingly.

			### 4. What are the limitations \[3’\] <mention-user url="{{user://1f9d872b-594c-8109-8371-00025aeaa944}}"/>  {toggle="true"}
				<synced_block_reference url="{{https://www.notion.so/2551ae2178f58002a47dc5c39af9c4d9#2651ae2178f5803f862bd564890b4896}}">
					<details>
					<summary>**Product Recommendation Timing**:</summary>
						- Merchants cannot block Shopping Assistant from Showcasing product recommendations until a given question is answered to.
						- Merchants cannot force the Shopping Assistant to display Product Cards in the first two iteration - except if they put the selling style to promotional. Otherwise, in educational and balanced we keep waiting the 2nd iteration before displaying product cards.
					</details>
					<details>
					<summary>**One Step at a time**: </summary>
						Merchants cannot force Shopping Assistant to ask one discovery question after another waiting for customer reply in between. This is also true for a guidance asking to share discounts one by one - it will share discounts too quickly. 
					</details>
					<details>
					<summary>**Excluded product**: </summary>
						If merchants exclude products using the new Product Recommendation feature and have a guidance promoting this product in a given scenario - Shopping Assistant will recommend the excluded product.
					</details>
					<details>
					<summary>**Out of Stock Products**: </summary>
						If Guidances recommend out of stock Products, those products will still be mentioned in the message but the product recommendation cards won’t be displayed. 
					</details>
					<details>
					<summary>**Block Discounts based on Customer Shopify Properties **(e.g. VIP Tag): </summary>
						Even though the guidance request not to share discount based on Customer’s tag this won’t impact the answer, because Customers tags are not shared within the pipeline.
					</details>
				</synced_block_reference>
