Executive Summary

The goal was to create a Help Center based on real data and conversations the Suspension Superstore team have been having with their customers. The Help Center goal is to reduce the customer support team's ticket volume while improving customer satisfaction by making it easy for visitors of the Suspension Store website to find answers to most common questions.

Suspension Superstore — Help Center Strategy 03

Help Center Structure

Based on ticket analysis, I designed a lean 5-category structure. Each category directly addresses the highest-volume customer intents.

Orders & Shipping
5 Articles
Addresses the #1 customer intent: "Where is my order?"
  • How do I track my order?
  • How long does shipping take?
  • Do you ship internationally?
  • My order hasn't shipped yet
  • Why is my order on hold?
Returns & Refunds
5 Articles
Clear policy explanation reduces back-and-forth.
  • How do I return a part?
  • What is your return policy?
  • Can I exchange for a different part?
  • How do I cancel my order?
  • How long do refunds take?
Product & Installation
4 Articles
Industry-specific content for automotive customers.
  • Will this part fit my vehicle?
  • Where are installation instructions?
  • How do I file a warranty claim?
  • Military/first responder discounts
Dealers & Wholesale
3 Articles
B2B customers represent significant ticket volume.
  • How do I become a dealer?
  • How do I update my tax exempt certificate?
  • Do you have a dealer portal?
Shipping Problems
3 Articles
Quick resolution for damaged, missing, or incorrect shipments.
  • My order arrived damaged
  • My order is missing parts
  • I received the wrong item

Why only 5 categories? Gorgias best practices recommend keeping Help Centers lean. More categories = harder navigation = lower deflection. These 5 categories cover 80%+ of deflectable intents.

Suspension Superstore — Help Center Strategy 04

Article Structure

Every article follows a consistent, scannable structure designed to give customers their answer fast.

Standard Article Format

How do I return a part?
"Start a return and get your RMA number."
Quick Answer
How It Works
Common Issues
Still Need Help?

Why This Structure Works

Element Why It Matters
Quick Answer First 70% of customers just need the short answer — put it at the top.
Step-by-Step Instructions Numbered lists reduce confusion and prevent follow-up tickets.
Common Issues Section Proactively addresses edge cases that generate tickets.
"Still Need Help?" Customers who contact support come prepared with the right info.
Customer Language Article titles use phrases from actual tickets, not internal jargon.

Industry-Specific Enhancements

  • Fitment guidance: Year/Make/Model/Trim requirements explained clearly
  • Warranty redirects: Contact info for manufacturers (MaxTrac, Belltech, Cognito, IHC)
  • Installation resources: Links to guides and manufacturer videos
  • 2WD vs 4WD callouts: Common fitment confusion addressed upfront

What Stays Agent-Only

These intents intentionally remain with your support team — they require human expertise:

  • Vehicle-specific fitment lookups — Requires expertise and database access
  • Product recommendations — Needs consultation based on customer goals
  • Dealer pricing negotiations — B2B relationships need human touch
  • Order modifications — Time-sensitive, requires account access
  • Complex return exceptions — Case-by-case judgment calls
